## AWS Bedrock: The Infrastructure Advantage

Amazon Bedrock transforms how organizations deploy foundation models by abstracting infrastructure complexity. Rather than managing API keys, rate limits, and custom integration code, enterprises access Claude through AWS's existing IAM, monitoring, and billing systems. This architectural decision reduces implementation time from months to weeks—a critical factor when evaluating AI investments.

The serverless model offers particular advantages for variable workloads. Organizations pay only for tokens processed rather than maintaining dedicated infrastructure. For businesses with seasonal demand patterns or pilot projects with uncertain scale, this eliminates the capital risk of over-provisioning. AWS reports that Bedrock customers typically see 40-60% lower total cost of ownership compared to self-hosted model deployments when accounting for engineering time, infrastructure management, and scaling overhead.

Security and compliance benefits matter equally. Bedrock inherits AWS's certification portfolio—including GDPR, SOC 2, ISO 27001, and region-specific standards. Data never leaves the customer's AWS environment, addressing data residency requirements that complicate direct API integrations. For Ukrainian enterprises serving EU clients, this compliance inheritance simplifies certification processes considerably.
